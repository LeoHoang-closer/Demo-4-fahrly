Most delivery operators hit a wall at 20–30 drivers. Revenue plateaus. Costs keep climbing. The instinct is to hire.

The real problem is route efficiency.

## The math that kills margins

A driver doing 22 stops per day instead of 28 sounds like a small gap. Multiply it by 30 drivers, 250 working days, and a revenue-per-stop of 4 €:

- Inefficient: 22 × 30 × 250 × 4 € = **660.000 €/year**
- Optimized: 28 × 30 × 250 × 4 € = **840.000 €/year**

That's 180.000 € in revenue from the same fleet, same headcount.

## Why manual planning fails at scale

Route planning by spreadsheet works for 5 drivers. At 30, the combinatorial complexity is beyond human calculation. A dispatcher spending 2 hours planning routes every morning is the bottleneck — not the drivers.

## What optimization actually does

Automated route optimization doesn't just find shorter paths. It accounts for:

- Time windows per stop
- Driver start/end locations
- Vehicle capacity
- Traffic patterns by hour

The result: more stops per hour, lower fuel cost, fewer late deliveries.

## The operator who fixed it

One operator in Munich ran 28 drivers on manual routes. Average: 21 stops/day. After switching to automated optimization: 27 stops/day. Same drivers. Same vehicles. Revenue up 28 % in 60 days.

The only change was the routing software.
